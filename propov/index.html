ProPOV: Open-Source Persistence of Vision Clock

ProPOV is an open-source miniature propeller clock and persistence of vision (POV) gadget. It uses surface-mount components on a custom PCB designed in gEDA.

Principle

Persistence of vision relies on the brain’s computational shortcuts to simulate an image ‘floating’ in the air. The ProPOV rotates quickly, flashing LEDs at very specific times such that a clock face appears in the air. The image is determined by the precise timing of the LED on- and off-time, based on instructions from the microcontroller.

ProPOV judges its position in a rotation by averaging rotational speed using a magnetic sensor looking for a stationary magnet positioned somewhere nearby. It keeps time using a Dallas/Maxim RTC (real-time clock) and a dedicated timing crystal of the same type found in wristwatches.
