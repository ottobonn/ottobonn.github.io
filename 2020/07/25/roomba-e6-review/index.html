Roomba e6 Review: Robots Won't Save Us

25 July, 2020

It’s the technologists’ dream of domestic bliss: a robot that cleans your house for you while you do other, more important things. It tucks itself in to charge and runs on a schedule. It takes care of stray cat hair and scattered litter. It’s one small step along a long continuum of automation, with the ultimate goal being (presumably) the flawless electromechanical incarnation of a servant.

The iRobot Roomba e6 is iRobot’s attempt at realizing this dream. The only problem is that it sucks.

Let’s be realistic

Look, I’m as optimistic as the next guy about the potential of computers and robotics, but I set my hopes realistically low for this battery-powered floor frisbee. This little consumer robot is one small toe forward on our halting, clumsy march towards robots we might think of as intelligent. If it’s a useful snapshot along the way of our research in its limited scope, it could be worthwile, even as we work toward something grander.

Here’s what I would hope for, at a minimum:

  • It should vacuum the floor, at least in places it can easily fit
  • without damaging things

No rugs allowed

I set up the cute little charging station under the couch, where it would be out of sight but where the Roomba would have a clear line of sight. There’s a rug under there, too, and that’s where the problems began.

The Roomba has a way to hike its body up over obstacles by extending its wheels, but when we turned it on it immediately ran into the edge of the rug and perceived it as a wall and not a traversible obstacle. It spent the next five minutes sigging and zagging in the 1-foot clearing between the wall behind the couch and this edge of the rug, running over its own charging cord in the process.

Okay, so it has trouble with rugs. We can live with that.

A maddeningly inept assistant

  • it routes randomly, resulting in a lot of wasted effort
  • it runs over things (beware of strings especially)
  • it scares cats
  • it’s very loud, and the noise in combination with its ineptitude means you won’t want to be home when it’s running
  • it can’t find its way home from other rooms
  • we have to put up light beacons?
  • it fills up instantly (but it isn’t really full)
