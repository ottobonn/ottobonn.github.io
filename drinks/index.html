drinks

A list of some thoughts on drinks. Cheers 🍻

Scotch

I don’t usually go for scotch because I find most of them too peaty. I have had some good ones though.

Kilchoman Mahir Bay Islay

Smokey. As. Fuck. You might like this but I find it a bit hard to drink, though it grows on me on subsequent encounters. I prefer my wildfire smoke airborne. Nice box though, and an NFC-enabled bottle?

Johnny Walker Black Label

Hot damn! This is good. It’s a bit on the pricey side but it’s just the right amount of smoke.

Japanese Whiskey

Akashi

My go-to favorite. Prices vary but I expect it to be around $50 for a fifth. Smooth, rich and imminently drinkable.

Suntori Hibiki “Japanese Harmony”

This is another good bottle but I don’t prefer it to the Akashi. I would say it takes second place.

Nikka Coffey Grain

It’s “coffey” for the type of still—fortunately not in the “Irish coffee” sense. This is a great bottle but expect to spend around $100 for a fifth.

Ryo

Trader Joe’s tends to carry this one. It is, as I like to say, “ethanol forward.” This is a light and thin-bodied whisky in all the wrong ways, and it comes across as harsh and almost toxically medicinal. I don’t recommend it.

Bourbon

I am from Kentucky, after all.

Wild Turkey 101

A guilty pleasure. This one is powerful. Great for sipping around the campfire or fueling the flames. Just don’t overdo it; you’ll be feeling this one more than your typical bourbon.

Knob Creek

A solid house bourbon to have on hand. I like it straight and in a Manhattan. I wish they would stop with the sticky black wax on their bottles.

Weller

Maker’s Mark

A classic. Consistent but a bit boring in my opinion.

Johnny Drum

The plastic bottle suggests a bargain, and this is a lot of bang for your buck. It’s a strong bourbon and makes for good cocktails. I haven’t seen it outside Kentucky, though.

Bulleit

Bulleit bourbon and rye are great house whiskeys to have on hand. They both have the warm brown sugar and vanilla I would expect but they get out of the way in cocktails and they’re cheap enough that I don’t regret mixing with them.

Rye

Templeton

This is an awesome Rye. Great for sipping or mixing.

Michter’s

Another classic. Do try it if you haven’t!

Sazerac

I do like a good sazerac cocktail, but I don’t go wild for this rye. It’s okay.

Slow & Low Rock & Rye

This shit is weird, but pretty good. Orange peels and sugar and rye in a bottle. I would definitely recommend serving it very cold to control the volatiles of the alcohol and orange that otherwise attack the nose.

Basil Hayden’s Carribean Rye

I bought this one after someone in a store broke a bottle and the wonderful smell filled the place. Unfortunately I don’t love the flavor. The rum and rye just taste like, well, rum and rye, and they don’t want to play well with each other. This one’s a “no” from me. But I do love their bourbon!

Vermouth

Carpano Antiqua

Case closed.

Gin

209

This SF-native bottle can be tricky to find but I really enjoy it. Lots of flowers with a smooth and strong body.

Hendricks

I was pleasantly suprised. This is what I hope for in a gin; it’s bright and fragrant and complex. Definitely recommend.

Beer

Where to begin… How about nowhere. I’ll be back to fill out this section. Time for a beer.
